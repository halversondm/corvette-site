Description Corvette
This is a 1953 Corvette.  Originally introduced with a six cylinder engine, the Corvette was quickly taken in by the American public
This is the 1963 Split Window Corvette.  This is a rare Corvette because the window in the rear is split by a section of fiberglass.  In 1964 the window was changed to a full window because of visibility problems
The 1982 Corvette marked the last of this generation of Corvettes.  Dating back to 1968, this style of Corvette went through the most change in terms of body, interior, engine performance and fuel efficiency.  Mostly though the body retained the "Mako Shark" shape which developed the 1968 Corvette.
The Fourth Generation of Corvette is marked by a plant location change and of course a body change.  The theme of the fourth generation is technology.  This is due to the use of fuel injection and computers along with emission controls.  Corvette production was moved from St. Louis to Bowling Green, Kentucky.  This is a 1994 Corvette ZR1.
The Fifth Generation or C5 is the most aggressive of all Corvettes made.  The Corvette has always been in the top for new technologies and this one shows no stopping of that.  With Active Handling Systems, Airbag technologies and of course the all new all-aluminum LS1 block.  Corvette is moving in the future.
